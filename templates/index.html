An interactive look at rat behavioral data from the paper:

Neural correlates of task switching in prefrontal cortex and primary auditory cortex in auditory cortex in a novel stimulus selection task for rodents

Contrary to our title we will not show you rodents running on a track, but we will provide you with visualization based on data from the work of Dr. Chris Rodgers and Dr. Michael DeWeese, investigating rat responses in a Go/No-Go task experiment. These data look specifically at rat behavioral responses, although the study itself goes further to explore the prefrontal cortex and primary auditory cortex.

The Go/No-Go rat paradigm is dependent on animals’ selective attention to target sounds despite static background. If you have ever heard of the cocktail party effect, this may sound familiar to you: the ability to hear your own name at a crowded party despite all the distractions. The researchers of this study hypothesize we can attribute this effect to the primary auditory cortex (A1) and the medial prefrontal cortex (mPFC) as previously mentioned.

This experiment uses 4 conditions to test this in rats: a either high pitched tone or a low pitched tone in pitch cue trials, or either a tone on the left or right side in localization trials.

When you pick a rat below, we will show their behavioral performace. We use a hit to mean a success. Miss means they performed the correct behavior but missed the correct option, and an error means they performed the wrong behavior.
